The Language of Seeing
观察的语言

The descriptive stance is a practice in not overforming what is given — allowing what appears to remain in its appearance. It is also a discipline for the coach: to hold back judgement, to set aside personal feeling, and to stay with what is actually there.

描述性的姿态是一种练习，让所见之物保持其本来面貌，而不加以过度诠释。这也是教练自律的训练：暂时搁置判断与个人感受，专注于眼前真实存在的事物。

Often, after a careful phenomenological description, we find a far more precise and chosen access to the artwork — and to the person who lives within it.

往往在细致的现象学描述之后，我们才能更精准、更有选择性地进入作品——以及居于其中的人。

Describe only what is visible. No interpretation yet. One sentence — one observation.
只描述可见之物。暂不诠释。一句话——一个观察。

Module 1 · 模块一
Safety Anchors in Motion
安全锚点的运动
What movement quality is visible in the marks? Circling, ordering, expanding, staying contained, condensing? Fluid, weighted, springing, forceful, floating?
笔迹中可见怎样的运动特质？盘旋、整理、扩展、保持收敛、凝缩？流动、沉重、跳跃、有力、飘浮？
Module 2 · 模块二
Space-Time Schema
时空图式
How is the available space used? Which areas are occupied, which are left open? Where does the activity concentrate — at the edges, at the centre, in one region?
如何使用可用空间？哪些区域被占据，哪些被留空？活动集中在哪里——边缘、中心，还是某一区域？
Module 4 · 模块四
Seeking in the Process
过程中的探索
Where in the picture is energy visible? What has been built, extended, covered, or left open? What does the sequence of marks suggest about the movement of attention?
画面中哪里可见能量？什么被建构、延伸、覆盖或留白？笔迹的顺序暗示了注意力怎样的流动？
